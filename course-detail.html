Learning outcomes

1
Explain prompting and assisting with medication and how this differs from medication administration.
2
Explain the role of a care assistant in medication administration in home care.
3
Recognise when a service user may require prompting or assistance with medication within the limits of your role.
4
Recognise signs of side-effects and adverse drug reactions in a service user.
5
Identify actions of how to respond if a service user refuses their medication.
6
Explain effective communication techniques when interacting with service users regarding medication administration.
7
Explain the importance of accurate medication records and documentation, including the proper recording of medication administration and any associated observations or concerns.
8
Identify the potential consequences of working outside of the limitations of your role in relation to medication practice.

About this course

Welcome to the Florence Prompting and assisting with medication in Home Care course. This module is designed to provide you with the essential knowledge required for the safe and effective practice of prompting and assisting with medication within home care in the UK. This course covers the basics you need to know to support service users to take medication in their homes. This course is CPD accredited, aligns with the relevant NICE guidelines, and meets the Skills for Care requirement for training in managing medication.

What is CPD?

Continuous Professional Development (CPD) is the way professionals continue to learn and develop throughout their career. Florence Academy e-learning modules are CPD accredited and can be used to evidence ongoing CPD hours. Most modules are independently accredited by the CPD Standards Office.

Will any care home accept my certificates?

Any care home or NHS Trust outside of Florence can accept them, depending on their own policies. We recommend checking directly with the organisation.
